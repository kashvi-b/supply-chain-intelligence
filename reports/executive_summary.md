## Executive Summary — NovaMart Global

### Analysis Period: January 2023 – December 2024

### Prepared by: Kashvi Bhardwaj , Supply Chain Analytics

---

## THE SITUATION

NovaMart Global processed **5,000 shipments** across multiple international trade routes during the 2023–2024 analysis period. The Supply Chain Intelligence Platform evaluated shipment reliability, disruption exposure, supplier concentration risk, and transportation efficiency across the company’s global logistics network. 

**Headline metrics:**

| KPI                               | Result    | Status                      |
| --------------------------------- | --------- | --------------------------- |
| On-Time Delivery Rate             | 82.9%     | ▼ Below 85% target          |
| Average Delay Duration            | 13.4 days | ▼ High                      |
| Shipments Impacted by Disruptions | 16.3%     | ▼ Increasing exposure       |
| Total Shipments                   | 5,000     | Stable                      |
| Total Value at Risk               | $453.5M   | ▼ High operational exposure |

Although overall delivery performance remains relatively stable, the company’s **82.9% OTD performance still falls below the 85% operational target**, indicating persistent supply chain inefficiencies and disruption vulnerability. 

---

## THE COMPLICATION

Three major operational risks are driving supply chain instability:

### Problem 1 — External Disruption Vulnerability

Six major disruption events significantly impacted shipment reliability during the analysis period. The most severe event, the **Red Sea Crisis (Severity 10/10)**, created major delays across Middle East and Asia-Europe routes. Additional disruptions including **Chinese New Year Congestion**, **Rotterdam Port Strike**, and **Taiwan Strait Tension** caused prolonged operational slowdowns and shipment volatility. 

The analysis shows that the organization currently operates with a largely reactive disruption management strategy, limiting the ability to reroute or prioritize shipments before delays escalate.

---

### Problem 2 — Tier 3 Supplier Concentration Risk

Supplier analysis identified elevated delay rates among Tier 3 suppliers operating in Asia-Pacific regions. Current sourcing allocation places approximately **32% of critical SKU orders with Tier 3 vendors**, creating excessive dependency on higher-risk suppliers. These vendors consistently recorded higher disruption-related delays compared to Tier 1 and Tier 2 suppliers. 

Without supplier diversification, the company remains vulnerable to regional geopolitical and operational instability.

---

### Problem 3 — Seasonal Logistics Congestion

The platform detected a recurring **Q1 seasonality pattern**, where January–February consistently experiences elevated delay rates due to Chinese New Year congestion and reduced transportation capacity. Historical shipment patterns indicate that these seasonal disruptions repeat annually across all years analyzed. 

Current inventory planning processes do not adequately compensate for these predictable disruption windows, increasing stockout and delay risks during Q1 operations.

---

## THE QUESTION

Given increasing disruption exposure, supplier concentration risk, and recurring seasonal congestion — **where should NovaMart prioritize operational investments to improve supply chain resilience and delivery performance?**

---

## THE RECOMMENDATIONS

Based on quantitative analysis across 5,000 shipments, the platform identifies three priority recommendations:

### Recommendation 1 — Deploy Predictive Disruption Monitoring

**Potential impact: Reduced disruption-related delays and faster operational response**

Implement real-time disruption monitoring workflows integrated with shipment risk scoring. High-risk events such as geopolitical crises, labor strikes, and weather disruptions should automatically trigger rerouting reviews and escalation workflows within 24 hours.

*Owner: VP Supply Chain | Timeline: Q1 2025*

---

### Recommendation 2 — Reduce Tier 3 Supplier Dependency

**Potential impact: Improved supplier reliability and reduced delay exposure**

Reduce dependency on Tier 3 Asia-Pacific suppliers by reallocating critical SKUs toward Tier 1 and Tier 2 vendors. Procurement teams should introduce stricter supplier performance reviews and contract-based improvement requirements for high-risk vendors.

*Owner: Procurement Head | Timeline: Q2 2025*

---

### Recommendation 3 — Activate Predictive Inventory Buffering

**Potential impact: Reduced Q1 stockout and congestion impact**

Implement seasonal inventory buffering before January–February disruption periods. The analysis recommends pre-positioning inventory before December 15 to reduce congestion-related shipment delays and improve product availability during Q1 demand spikes.

*Owner: Warehouse Operations | Timeline: Q3 2025*
